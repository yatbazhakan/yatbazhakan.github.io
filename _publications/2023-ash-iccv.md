In this paper, the focus is on improving the introspection mechanisms—also known as self-assessment—for deep neural network (DNN)-based object detection in Automated Driving Systems (ADS). Specifically, the study challenges the efficacy of using raw neural activation patterns for introspecting 2D object detection failures. The paper introduces an optimized pre-processing approach for these activation patterns to enhance error detection performance. Evaluation on the Berkeley Deep Drive (BDD) dataset reveals that the pre-processed activation patterns result in a 14% reduction in missed errors and a 3% improvement in overall detection performance when compared with a state-of-the-art algorithm.
